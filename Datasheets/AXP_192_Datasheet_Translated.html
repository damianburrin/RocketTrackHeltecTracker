1. Summary
AXP192 is a highly integrated power system management chip, aimed at single-cell lithium batteries (lithium ion or lithium polymer) and requires multiple power conversion
The output application provides a simple and easy-to-use complete power supply solution that can be flexibly configured to fully meet the current increasingly complex application processing
The power supply system requires relatively complex and precise control of the power supply.
AXP192 integrates an adaptive USB-Compatible charger, 3-channel buck converter (Buck DC-DC
converter), 4 linear regulators (LDO), voltage/current/temperature monitoring and other multiple 12-Bit ADCs. To ensure the safety and stability of the power supply system,
AXP192 also integrates over/under voltage (OVP/UVP), over temperature (OTP), over current (OCP) and other protection circuits.
The Intelligent Power Select (IPS™) circuit of AXP192 can be connected to USB, external AC adapter, lithium battery
Safe and transparent distribution of electric energy between the battery and the application system load, and when there is only external input power and no battery (or battery over-discharge/damage)
Under the circumstances, the application system can also work normally.
AXP192 has three input capabilities such as external adapter, USB and battery, and supports rechargeable backup batteries.
AXP192 provides a two-wire serial communication interface for communication with the host: Two Wire Serial Interface (TWSI), the application processor can
This interface can be used to turn on or turn off certain power outputs, set their voltages, access internal registers and various measurement data (including Fuel
Gauge). The high-precision (0.5%) power measurement data facilitates consumers to grasp the status of power usage more clearly in real time, which will bring consumers forward
Unprecedented equipment power usage experience.
AXP192 is available in a 6mm x 6mm 48-pin QFN package.

9. Control and Operating
When the AXP192 is working, the SCK/SDA pin of the TWSI interface is pulled up to the system IO power supply, and then the Host can connect to the AXP192 through this interface.
Flexible adjustment and monitoring of the working status of the user can obtain a wealth of information.
Note: "Host" refers to the main processor of the application system.
Note: The "external power supply" referred to below includes ACIN and VBUS inputs.
9.1 Work Mode and Reset (Power On/Off & Reset)
Working mode button (PEK)
A key can be connected between the PWRON pin of AXP192 and GND as an independent power enable key (PEK)
Or sleep/wake button. AXP192 can automatically recognize the "long press" and "short press" of this button and respond accordingly.
Several boot sources (Power on Source)
1. ACIN, VBUS and battery access.
2. N_OE changes from high to low.
3. PEK.
Power On—Method A
When the SYSEN pin and LDO1 are connected together, AXP192 is in switch mode A.
When N_OE is low, when the required main power supply (ACIN or VBUS>3.8V, battery voltage is higher than the shutdown voltage) is connected, AXP192
It will be automatically turned on (whether to automatically turn on when the external power supply is connected can be rewritten according to external requirements).
When N_OE is low and in the shutdown state, the startup action needs to be completed by PEK operation.
In the case of external power supply or battery, the change of N_OE from high to low will also cause AXP192 to boot.
AXP192 can be powered on by PEK (the time of pressing the key exceeds "ONLEVEL"). In actual application, the timing (Alarm) output signal of Host
The signal can also be connected to PWRON—connect with PEK in parallel. When the Alarm signal is valid (low level), it is equivalent to pressing PEK, or you can press AXP192
Turn on.
After power-on, DC-DC and LDO will be soft-started according to the set sequence. After the start-up, it can be managed by Host or through PWREN.
Pin turns on/off the corresponding power supply.
Power Off—Method A
When the PEK "long press" time is longer than IRQLEVEL, in the PEK interrupt service routine, Host can set the "register REG32H[7]"
Write "1" to notify AXP192 to enter the shutdown state. When AXP192 enters the shutdown state, all power outputs except LDO1 will be turned off.

Under the following conditions, AXP192 will automatically shut down:
1. The input voltage is too low and low power protection;
2. Excessive load causes the output voltage of the power supply to be too low and overload protection;
3. The input voltage is too high and overvoltage protection (see the "Power Path Management" chapter for details);
4. N_OE changes from low to high without shutting down within the set time;
5. When PEK is greater than OFFLEVEL (default 6S), the system will automatically turn off other outputs except LDO1;
